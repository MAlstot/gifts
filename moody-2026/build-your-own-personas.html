Build Your Own Personas — Customization Template

The seven personas in the Assignment Integrity Stress Test are research-backed composites. But you know your students. This template lets you replace or extend the personas with students from your course — which makes the stress test dramatically more honest.

Where to get the raw material: your learner information survey (confidence, anxiety, time pressures, which AI tools they actually use and how), patterns from past semesters, and your discipline's specific pressure points (licensure exams, portfolio reviews, cohort dynamics, working-professional schedules).

Privacy note (Diligence): personas are composites, never real, identifiable students. No names, no identifying details, and never paste real student data — survey responses included — into an unprotected AI tool. Use Baylor's protected Copilot, and aggregate before you paste (e.g., "40% of my students report low confidence in academic writing").

The persona recipe

Every persona needs four ingredients. Fill in one card per persona:

PERSONA NAME: The ___________________
  (A memorable label for the pressure, not the person)

ROOTED IN (pick 1–2 of the eleven reasons):
  self-efficacy · expectations · values · moral disengagement ·
  contract cheating · peer effects · narcissism · motivation ·
  procrastination · instructor effects · cost-benefit

BAD DAY: One or two sentences. What is true for this student the week
  your assignment is due? (Time, confidence, competing obligations,
  what they believe about the rules, what tools are open in the
  other tab.)

WHAT THEY SCAN FOR: What in the assignment text will this student
  read closely — and what gap or ambiguity will they exploit or
  fall into? Be specific to how they'd misuse AI.

Worked example (graduate, online, working professionals)

PERSONA NAME: The Sunday-Night Director

ROOTED IN: procrastination + cost-benefit

BAD DAY: Full-time K-12 administrator in an online EdD. It's Sunday,
  9 PM, the discussion post is due at midnight, and a board meeting
  ate the weekend. Copilot is already open from work.

WHAT THEY SCAN FOR: Whether anyone will actually read this post
  closely. Whether the prompt could be answered without the readings.
  If the post only needs to "sound right," AI writes it in 40 seconds
  — and next week's post, too.

Prompts to help you draft (paste into your AI with survey aggregates)

  • "Here are anonymized, aggregated patterns from my learner survey: [paste]. Draft 3 persona cards using the recipe above, each rooted in different reasons from the eleven, specific to a [discipline] course delivered [modality]."
  • "My discipline's specific integrity pressure points are: [licensure, clinical hours, cohort culture...]. Which of the eleven reasons do these amplify, and what persona would carry them?"

Swapping personas into the stress test

In the stress-test prompt (or Skill), replace any of the seven default personas with yours — but keep the coverage rule: across your final persona set, all eleven reasons must appear at least once. A quick tally table helps:

Reason Covered by
Self-efficacy
Expectations
Values
Moral disengagement
Contract cheating
Peer effects
Narcissism
Motivation
Procrastination
Instructor effects
Cost-benefit

Keep the three failure layers and the severity/rebuild steps unchanged — the personas are the lens; the machinery is the test.
