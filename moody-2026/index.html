Set your stance, then your rails

Small teaching moves 3 & 5

  • AI Literacy Continuum self-evaluation. The interactive from our quiet-time moment: place your course now, set a target for one assignment, and see the gap — it's your growth edge, not your failure.
  • Guardrail Starters — an interactive. Pick your assignment's AI literacy level (No AI Use → AI Innovation), get copy-ready guardrail language for all four Ds — each rail with the student-facing why, plus RADAR-style student rails and the master guardrail. Starters, not policy: adapt, then stress test. New in Step 4: a Copilot skill builder — the four-part recipe (Name · Input · Workflow · Constraints), a worked example, your stress test pre-written as a level-matched skill, and a blank recipe for building your own.

Stress test your assignment

Small teaching move 4 · the hero takeaway

  • The Assignment Integrity Stress Test. Works in Baylor's protected Copilot (look for the green shield), Claude, or any chat AI. Seven students on a bad day, built from the eleven reasons, run against your assignment. Two paths on one page: quick (copy the full prompt) or guided (answer five questions and get a personalized prompt with your level, kernel, and student context pre-loaded).
  • Claude Skill version (SKILL.md) — for Claude users: add it once, then just say “stress test this assignment.”
  • Build-your-own-personas template — swap in composites of your students for a far more honest test. (markdown download)
Test where your students live. A rail that holds in Copilot can leak in the free tier of another tool. Your learner survey tells you which tool and version to test in.
