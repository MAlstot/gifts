Design Prompt Starters

Move 5 · Act on what the stress test revealed — with AI as a thinking partner, not an author.

The stress test diagnoses; it deliberately doesn't fix. That decision is yours. Use these starters to think through a revision AI can help you shape — but you read the diagnosis, you choose the direction, and you keep the pen. Each prompt asks AI for options with tradeoffs, never a finished assignment.

Run it in Baylor's protected Copilot (green shield) so your materials stay institutionally protected — the same Diligence move we ask of students. Fill the [brackets] first.

1 · Make the thinking visible

When the stress test flagged: the kernel can be offloaded with no visible change (Kernel surrender).

Act as an instructional-design thinking partner. My assignment is [paste it]. The thinking it exists to build is [your kernel sentence]. The stress test flagged that a student could offload that thinking invisibly. Propose 3 different ways to break this into low-stakes checkpoints that make student thinking visible along the way — an outline, a decision memo, an annotated source, a short voice note, whatever fits [my discipline]. For each, give the tradeoff. Do not rewrite my assignment; give me options and I'll choose.

2 · Build discipline-specific ethical scenarios

When you want students to practice judgment instead of being handed a rule.

Create 5 realistic, discipline-specific ethical dilemmas about AI use that a student in [field] might actually face, tied to the context of [this assignment]. Keep them open-ended — no obvious right answer — so they work as discussion prompts. For each, note which of the tensions it surfaces (e.g., disclosure, authorship, verification, equity). I'll select and adapt.

3 · Surface the ethics already in your objectives

When you want the integrity conversation to grow from the learning, not feel bolted on.

Here are my learning objectives: [paste them]. Where are ethical or integrity considerations already nested in these objectives, and what real-world professional outcomes do they map to? Show me the connections you see; I'll decide which to make explicit for students.

4 · Rethink the assessment form

When the stress test flagged: the format itself invites offloading (a product-only deliverable).

The stress test flagged [the weakness you chose to address] in my [current format, e.g., final essay]. Give me 8–10 alternative ways to assess [the objective] — and for each, explain how it changes the integrity pressure (does it make thinking visible? distribute stakes? require the student's own context?). Rank none of them; I'll weigh them against what I know about my students.
